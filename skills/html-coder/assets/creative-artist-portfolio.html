Selected Works

Memory Fragments Series

2025-2026

Mixed media on canvas, incorporating found objects, photography transfers, and acrylic paint

Various dimensions, 24" x 36" to 48" x 72"

This ongoing series explores the fractured nature of memory—how our recollections layer, fade, and transform over time. Each piece incorporates personal photographs, vintage ephemera, and textured materials that represent the physical traces of lived experience.

The work investigates how we construct identity from fragments of the past, questioning the reliability of memory while celebrating its emotional resonance.

Status: Selected pieces available. Inquire for details

Threshold Series

2024

Steel, fabric, resin, and LED lighting

Site-specific installations, dimensions variable

Threshold examines liminal spaces—both physical and psychological—where transformation occurs. These sculptural installations create portals that viewers can move through, experiencing shifts in light, texture, and spatial perception.

The series draws inspiration from traditional architectural elements reimagined through contemporary materials, inviting contemplation about boundaries, passage, and change.

Collection: San Francisco Museum of Modern Art (commissioned installation)

Dialogue Series

2023

Oil and acrylic on linen, diptych format

Each panel: 36" x 48"

Presented as paired paintings, Dialogue explores the relationship between Eastern and Western aesthetic traditions, drawing from my Chinese-American heritage. Each diptych places abstract gestural marks in conversation with more structured, geometric forms.

The work examines cultural hybridity and the constant negotiation between different ways of seeing and being in the world.

Status: Series complete. Several pieces in private collections, 3 available for purchase

Ephemera Collection

2022-ongoing

Ink, watercolor, collage on handmade paper

8" x 10" to 12" x 16"

A more intimate body of work created as daily practice pieces. These smaller works explore spontaneity and imperfection, incorporating accidental marks, tea stains, and found papers collected during travels.

The collection celebrates the beauty of transience and the creative power of embracing mistakes.

Status: Available through studio visits. Prices range $400-$1,200

About the Artist

Maya Chen (b. 1992, San Francisco, CA) is a contemporary visual artist working primarily in mixed media, painting, and sculptural installation. Her work explores themes of cultural identity, memory, and transformation through layered, tactile compositions that invite prolonged contemplation.

Drawing from her Chinese-American heritage, Chen's practice investigates the spaces between cultures—the negotiations, translations, and hybrid forms that emerge from living across multiple worlds. Her work often incorporates found materials, vintage photographs, and traditional craft techniques alongside contemporary media.

Chen received her MFA from California College of the Arts (2018) and her BA from Yale University (2014). She has been awarded residencies at Headlands Center for the Arts and the Vermont Studio Center, and her work has been featured in Artforum, Art in America, and Hyperallergic.

She currently lives and works in Oakland, California.

Artist Statement

My work emerges from the intersection of memory, materiality, and cultural identity. As a first-generation Chinese-American, I've always been fascinated by the ways we construct meaning across different cultural frameworks—the translations, mistranslations, and beautiful hybrid forms that result.

I work primarily with mixed media and installation, building up layers of material that mirror the layering of experience and memory. My practice incorporates found photographs, vintage papers, textiles, and traditional craft techniques alongside contemporary materials like resin and LED lighting. This juxtaposition of old and new, East and West, reflects my lived experience navigating multiple cultural identities.

Memory—both personal and collective—is central to my work. I'm interested in how memories fragment, shift, and reconstruct over time, and how physical objects can serve as anchors for recollection. The materials I choose carry their own histories and associations, adding layers of meaning beyond my own intentions.

Through my art, I create spaces for reflection and questioning. I want viewers to slow down, look closely, and discover the subtle details and layered narratives embedded within each piece. My work asks: How do we remember? What gets preserved and what fades? How do we honor multiple truths simultaneously?

Ultimately, I believe art can create bridges—between cultures, between past and present, between individual experience and collective understanding. My hope is that the work resonates not just with those who share my specific cultural background, but with anyone who has navigated the complexity of identity, memory, and belonging.
